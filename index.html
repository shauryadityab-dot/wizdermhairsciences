Our Core Procedures

Explore our advanced, carefully vetted treatments designed to restore your hair naturally with maximum efficiency.

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) is the modern gold standard for patients seeking a permanent solution to hair loss without the need for traditional linear stitching. During this procedure, individual hair follicles are meticulously harvested from the donor area (typically the back of the head) using a precision micro-punch tool. Because the grafts are extracted one by one, the donor site heals with tiny, nearly invisible dots, allowing you to wear your hair short without any visible scarring.

Our surgeon-led FUE approach focuses on high-density packing and natural hairline design. By carefully calculating the exit angle and depth of every graft, we ensure that the transplanted hair mimics your natural growth pattern. This method offers a significantly faster recovery time compared to older techniques, making it an ideal choice for busy professionals looking for maximum results with minimal downtime.

Direct Hair Transplantation (DHT) is an advanced modification of the FUE technique designed to maximise graft viability and minimise the "out-of-body" time for hair follicles. Unlike standard procedures where grafts are extracted and stored for hours before implantation, the DHT method involves a simultaneous extraction and placement process. By utilising specialised implanter pens, our surgeons can control the depth, direction, and angle of each follicle with extreme precision, leading to a significantly higher survival rate for every graft.

This non-surgical intervention is highly effective for patients in the early stages of androgenetic alopecia or those experiencing telogen effluvium (temporary shedding). PRP is often used as a standalone therapy or as a supportive treatment following a transplant to accelerate healing and boost the diameter of existing hair shafts.

PRP Therapy is a revolutionary regenerative treatment that harnesses the healing power of your own blood to reverse hair thinning. The process involves drawing a small sample of your blood, which is then processed in a high-speed centrifuge to isolate a concentrated layer of platelets. These platelets are "powerhouses" of growth factors that, when injected into the scalp, stimulate dormant follicles, improve blood circulation, and prolong the active growth phase of the hair cycle.

This non-surgical intervention is highly effective for patients in the early stages of androgenetic alopecia or those experiencing telogen effluvium (temporary shedding). PRP is often used as a standalone therapy or as a supportive treatment following a transplant to accelerate healing and boost the diameter of existing hair shafts.

Growth Factor Concentrate (GFC) represents the next generation of hair restoration technology, offering a more potent and refined alternative to traditional PRP. While PRP contains various blood cells, GFC is a highly concentrated, acellular preparation that specifically extracts only the essential growth factors from your platelets. Because it is a "pure" concentrate, it delivers a much higher dose of regenerative proteins directly to the hair root, leading to faster and more visible improvements in hair volume and scalp health.

One of the standout benefits of GFC is the enhanced patient comfort; because it is acellular (free of red and white blood cells), the risk of post-treatment inflammation and pain is significantly reduced. Patients typically report a noticeable reduction in hair fall within just two to three sessions. GFC is particularly recommended for individuals who want a highly standardised treatment with predictable, high-performance results for thinning crowns and receding hairlines.

Hair Threads, or PDO (Polydioxanone) threads, are an innovative non-surgical treatment designed to mechanically and biologically stimulate hair growth. Fine, medical-grade dissolvable threads are inserted just beneath the surface of the scalp into the thinning areas. These threads act as a scaffold, immediately improving blood flow to the area and triggering a "wound healing" response that stimulates the production of collagen and fibroblasts around the hair follicles.

Over the course of six months, the threads naturally dissolve, but the biological stimulation they provide continues to strengthen the scalp's vascular network. This process revitalises miniaturised follicles and provides a firmer "grip" for the hair roots, resulting in thicker hair and a rejuvenated scalp environment. It is an excellent adjunctive therapy for patients who have not seen sufficient results from topical medications or light therapies alone.
